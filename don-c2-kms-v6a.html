THE THREE-BODY PROBLEM
Naval Aviation OT/CPS Cyber Incident Response — Authority Void Analysis
Framing: Root Cause Analysis | FMECA | Fleet Impact  |  Lens: NCA → SECNAV → CNO/OPNAV → NAVAIR Mission → Fleet
🎯
LEADERSHIP VIEW — Consequence Framing
You are looking at a category of risk that exists right now in your enterprise that no single authority chain owns. When the problem manifests, it will look like a maintenance discrepancy. It is not.
⚙️
PRACTITIONER VIEW — Navigation Framing
You already know this gap exists. This panel documents the authority boundaries, the incident response chain that should exist, and the navigation path available right now within current authority structures.
BODY 1
NAVAIR Technical Authority
PMA → Platform → RMF/ATO
Authority Basis: OPNAVINST 5450.350B — CNO delegates TA for Naval Aviation to NAVAIRSYSCOM. PMA holds airworthiness and configuration authority for each platform.
Cyber Posture: Risk Management Framework (RMF) / Authority to Operate (ATO). Framework designed for DODIN-connected IT systems — servers, workstations, networked devices.
⚠ THE GAP: Naval Aviation OT/CPS — avionics computers, mission systems, flight control, weapons systems, ground support equipment — are explicitly non-DODIN-connected by design. RMF/ATO was built for IT. Applying it to OT/CPS produces compliance theater: the boxes get checked, the ATO is current, the actual threat surface is never mapped.
Leadership Implication: Your program office has an ISSM with a binder and a current ATO. That ATO documents compliance with a framework that does not address the attack surface that adversaries are actively developing capabilities against. The binder is real. The protection it implies is not.
Practitioner Note: RMF controls applicable to OT/CPS: NIST SP 800-82 (ICS security) exists but is not systematically applied in Naval Aviation PMA program offices. NAVAIR has no enterprise OT/CPS cyber incident response procedure. The ISSM role was built for IT. When the OT incident occurs, the ISSM will escalate to the PMA. The PMA will escalate to NAVAIR. NAVAIR will call FLTCYBERCOM. See Body 2.

BODY 2
FLTCYBERCOM / DCO
DODIN Mission → Boundary Stop
Authority Basis: OPNAVINST 5450.345 — CNO designates FLTCYBERCOM as central operational authority for Navy networks. OPCON: CYBERCOM. Mission: Defensive Cyber Operations (DCO) for the DODIN.
Mandate Boundary: FLTCYBERCOM DCO authority applies to the DODIN. Naval Aviation OT/CPS systems exist outside the DODIN boundary — by the same design decisions that NAVAIR TA approved. This is not a gap that FLTCYBERCOM created. It is a gap that the architecture created.
⚠ THE GAP: When NAVAIR calls FLTCYBERCOM about a compromised avionics system, FLTCYBERCOM has no statutory authority to respond. They can advise. They cannot command. The DCO chain stops at the DODIN boundary. OT/CPS is on the other side of that boundary, alone.
Leadership Implication: You have a cyber warfare command. You have a platform authority. Neither owns the space where the aircraft's brain lives. This is not a resourcing problem. It is an authority structure problem. The solution requires deliberate action at the CNO/SECNAV level to create the authority that currently does not exist.
Practitioner Note: Current navigation path: NAVAIR CIO/CISO (N6 equivalent) has informal coordination with FLTCYBERCOM N3 for OT issues. This is relationship-based, not authority-based. For a significant OT/CPS incident at a deployed unit, the actual path is: unit CO → FRC → PMA → NAVAIR N6 → informal FLTCYBERCOM coordination → CYBERCOM advisory support. Each handoff loses time and authority. No one in this chain can compel action from anyone else in a different authority stream.

BODY 3
The Operational Commander
Mission Owner → Aircraft Down → No Chain to Call
Authority Basis: SecDef → CCDR → Numbered Fleet → CSG/MEF/ARG. Operational commanders own the mission. They do not own the platform. They do not own the network. They own the outcome.
The Incident: An aircraft cannot complete a mission because something in its OT layer — a mission computer, an avionics bus, a ground support system — behaved anomalously in a way that suggests adversary manipulation or exploitation. The aircraft is grounded. The mission is aborted.
🎯 NORTH STAR — THE ROOT CAUSE: The operational commander calls the FRC. The FRC calls the PMA. The PMA runs an ISSM checklist designed for a networked server. Nobody calls FLTCYBERCOM because nobody is certain that's the right call — and they're right to be uncertain, because it isn't clearly in FLTCYBERCOM's authority. The incident closes as a maintenance discrepancy. The adversary's access — if present — is neither confirmed nor denied. The next aircraft goes up.
Leadership Implication (National Command Authority lens): This scenario — aircraft grounded by an unexplained OT/CPS anomaly, resolved as a maintenance write-up, adversary access unresolved — is not hypothetical. The dormancy mechanism is ignorance, not absence. The gap between what your authority structure covers and what your adversary is targeting grows every day that no one owns this problem. The CNO's signature on OPNAVINST 5450.350B established NAVAIR TA over Naval Aviation platforms. That TA does not include a cyber incident response chain for OT/CPS. That chain does not exist. That is a fact that needs to be visible at every level from PMA to SECNAV.
Practitioner Navigation Path (current authorities, no new policy required):
  1. Immediate: Treat as flight safety discrepancy — grounds aircraft, triggers PMA technical authority chain.
  2. Concurrent: Notify command ISSO/ISSM — triggers RMF event log even if framework is inadequate. Creates paper trail.
  3. Parallel: CO contacts numbered fleet N6 / FLTCYBERCOM liaison. No authority to compel action but creates advisory relationship and documents the incident in a cyber context.
  4. NAVAIR path: PMA Program Manager → NAVAIR N6 → NAVAIR Commander. This is the TA chain. Use it. NAVAIR Commander can engage FLTCYBERCOM VADM directly outside formal authority chains.
  5. Gap documentation: Every incident that closes as a maintenance discrepancy when cyber causation cannot be ruled out should be documented as such. This is the data that will eventually force the policy action.
  6. MARFORCYBER note (USMC): For USMC units, MARFORCYBER (not FLTCYBERCOM) is the SCC. Same gap applies — MARFORCYBER DCO mandate is DODIN-bounded. But MARFORCYBER has a more direct relationship with USMC aviation units than FLTCYBERCOM does with Navy units. Use that relationship.

Root Cause Analysis — Why This Gap Exists
NCA / SECNAV
Cyber as warfare domain established by policy (10 U.S.C. §394). DODIN as the bounded operational space. OT/CPS not explicitly addressed in foundational cyber authority.
▼
CNO / OPNAV
OPNAVINST 5450.345 (2012) designates FLTCYBERCOM as central authority for Navy networks — DODIN-bounded. OPNAVINST 5450.350B (2022) establishes NAVAIR TA for Naval Aviation — RMF/ATO compliance, no OT/CPS cyber IR chain. These two instructions do not address the space between them.
▼
NAVAIR Mission, Functions & Tasks
NAVAIR executes TA through PMA program offices. Each PMA holds an ISSM position. ISSMs are trained on RMF for DODIN-connected systems. The OT/CPS security framework (NIST SP 800-82, IEC 62443) exists but is not operationalized in NAVAIR PMA standard practices. TA compliance = DODIN IT compliance. OT/CPS is outside the compliance framework by default.
▼
⚡ THE VOID — Where Authority Ends
Naval Aviation OT/CPS: avionics, mission computers, EW systems, weapons computers, flight control systems, ground support OT, intermediate maintenance systems. None connected to DODIN. All governed by TA frameworks designed for airworthiness, not cyber warfare. All outside FLTCYBERCOM DCO authority. All operated by commands whose cyber chain (FLTCYBERCOM or MARFORCYBER) has no visibility into this layer.
▼
Fleet Impact
The aircraft that cannot fly because of an OT/CPS anomaly closes as a maintenance gripe. The adversary's access — confirmed or not — is neither documented in the cyber domain nor investigated by anyone with cyber authority. The sortie generation number goes down. The leadership layer sees a readiness issue, not a cyber warfare event. The gap compounds silently. This is happening now.
