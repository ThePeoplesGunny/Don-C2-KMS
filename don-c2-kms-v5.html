SECTION 03

New Line Type — LCSP Configuration Data Return Path

The TA line (lime ↓, diamond ◇) represents authority from PM to operator. A second relationship must be represented: the mandatory return of maintenance data, configuration changes, and deficiency reports from operating/maintenance activities back to the PM. 10 USC § 4324 requires PM to maintain configuration currency throughout the system life cycle — this is not optional.

STATUTORY BASIS
  • 10 USC § 4324(b)(1)(G): Requires technical data and IP management plan for product support — PM must have the data to execute this.
  • 10 USC § 4324(b)(1)(H–I): Sustainment metrics, materiel availability, reliability tracking — all require continuous data return from the operating force.
  • COMNAVAIRFORINST 4790.2E, Ch. 5: MDS — maintenance actions, man-hours, and discrepancies at all levels captured and reported upward.
  • COMNAVAIRFORINST 4790.2E, Ch. 8: Aircraft logbooks and records — configuration data held by operating activities reported to PM/SYSCOM. This is the data NAVAIR holds as platform designer.

SECTION 04

Three Maintenance Levels as Navigable Architecture

COMNAVAIRFORINST 4790.2E defines three levels. These are capability designations, not organizational echelons — they may be co-located (MALS performs both O and I; depot FRCs perform both I and D). Each maintenance-capable node will display a level badge.
